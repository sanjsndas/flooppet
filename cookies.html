Cookies Policy

How we use cookies at FloopPet

1. Introduction

FloopPet (“we”, “our”, “us”) uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website to enhance your experience, analyze traffic, and personalize content. This Cookies Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, your choices regarding cookies, and how you can manage your preferences.

By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy. You can change your cookie preferences at any time by using the cookie settings dialog or by adjusting your browser settings. This policy is part of our broader commitment to transparency and user control, as outlined in our Privacy Policy.

We may update this Cookies Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, regulation, or our practices. The latest version will always be available on this page. Please review it periodically.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, smartphone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and give website owners information about how their site is being used.

Cookies can be “persistent” or “session” cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period of time or until you delete them, while session cookies are deleted automatically when you close your browser.

We use both types of cookies to ensure our website functions properly, remembers your preferences, and helps us understand how visitors interact with our content.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

We categorize cookies based on their purpose and function. Below is a detailed breakdown of the cookies we use:

3.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function properly. They enable core features such as security, network management, and accessibility. Without these cookies, our website would not work correctly, and you would not be able to use certain services (e.g., booking appointments, making payments).

  • Session cookies: Maintain your session ID while you navigate the site.
  • Security cookies: Help detect and prevent fraudulent activity.
  • Load balancing cookies: Distribute traffic across servers for optimal performance.

These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information and are typically set in response to actions you take, such as logging in or filling out forms.

3.2 Preference Cookies

Preference cookies allow us to remember your choices and preferences to provide a more personalized experience. For example, they remember your language preference, your pet’s breed and size (for quick booking), and your preferred grooming services.

  • Language preferences: Saves your selected language.
  • UI customization: Remembers font size, color themes, or layout choices.
  • Pet profile: If you have created a pet profile, these cookies help us recall it for future visits.

3.3 Analytics and Performance Cookies

We use analytics cookies to collect information about how visitors use our website. This helps us improve our content, navigation, and overall user experience. We use tools like Google Analytics and our own internal analytics to track metrics such as page views, bounce rates, and referral sources.

  • Google Analytics: Tracks visitor behavior in an anonymized manner. We have enabled IP anonymization to protect your privacy.
  • Performance cookies: Help us identify which pages are most popular and where users encounter errors.
  • Heatmap cookies: Occasionally used to understand user interaction patterns (e.g., clicks, scrolls).

All data collected is aggregated and does not personally identify you. We use this information to make data-driven decisions to enhance our services.

3.4 Marketing and Advertising Cookies

We may use marketing cookies to deliver relevant advertisements and promotional content. These cookies track your browsing habits and enable us to show you ads that are more likely to be of interest to you. We use them sparingly and in compliance with privacy regulations.

  • Retargeting cookies: Show you FloopPet ads on other websites you visit (e.g., Facebook, Google Display Network).
  • Social media cookies: Allow you to share content on social platforms (e.g., Facebook, Instagram).
  • Affiliate tracking cookies: Used if you click on a link from a partner website.

You can opt out of these cookies at any time through our cookie settings or your browser preferences.

4. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential website functions: To ensure our booking system, payment gateway, and user authentication work seamlessly.
  • Analytics and improvement: To understand how users interact with our website and identify areas for improvement.
  • Personalization: To remember your preferences and provide a tailored experience (e.g., recalling your pet’s grooming history).
  • Marketing: To show you relevant ads and measure the effectiveness of our campaigns.
  • Security: To protect our website and users from malicious activity.

We never use cookies to collect sensitive personal information without your explicit consent. We also do not use cookies to track you across unrelated websites for purposes other than those described above.

5. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our website are set by third-party services we use to enhance functionality. These include:

  • Google Analytics: Provides anonymized traffic data and user behavior insights.
  • Facebook Pixel: Used for retargeting and measuring ad performance on Facebook.
  • YouTube: If we embed videos, YouTube may set cookies to track viewing preferences.
  • Payment processors: Stripe, PayPal, or others may set cookies for secure transaction processing.
  • Social media buttons: Platforms like Instagram and Facebook may set cookies when you interact with their buttons.

We do not control third-party cookies, and we encourage you to review the privacy policies of these third-party providers. We only use third-party services that are GDPR and CCPA compliant.

6. Managing and Deleting Cookies

You have the right to accept or decline cookies at any time. You can manage your cookie preferences in the following ways:

  • Cookie Consent Dialog: Use the dialog box at the bottom of this page to accept or decline non-essential cookies.
  • Browser Settings: Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can block, delete, or allow cookies from specific sites. Instructions for popular browsers:
    • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data.
    • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data.
    • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data.
    • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions.
  • Opt-Out Tools: You can use tools like the Digital Advertising Alliance opt-out or Your Online Choices to manage advertising cookies.

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website, such as preventing you from booking appointments or accessing personalized features.

7. Consent and Withdrawal

When you first visit our website, we will display a cookie consent dialog that allows you to choose which types of cookies you accept. You can change your preferences at any time by clicking the “Cookie Settings” link in the footer or using the dialog at the bottom of this page.

If you initially accept cookies but later wish to withdraw consent, you can do so by adjusting your settings. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

We respect your choices and do not use cookies that require consent without your explicit permission.
